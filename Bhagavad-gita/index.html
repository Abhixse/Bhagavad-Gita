CHAPTER ONE

Observing the Armies on the Battlefield of Kuruksetra

As the opposing armies stand poised for battle,Arjuna,the mighty warrior,
sees his intimate relatives,teachers and friends in both armies ready to fight and secrifice their lives.
Overcome by grief and pity,Arjuna fails in strength ,his mind becomes bewildered,and he give up his determination to fight.

CHAPTER TWO

Contents of the Gita Summarized

Arjun Submit to Lord Krsna as his disciple ,and Krsna being His teachings to
Arjuna by explaning the fundamental distinction between the temporary material body and the eternal spritual soul.
the Lord explain the process of transmigration, the nature of selfless services to the Supreme and the chharacteristics of self-realized person.

CHAPTER THREE

Karma-yoga

Everyone is engage in some sort of activity in this material world.
But action can either bind one to this world or liberate one from it. By Acting for the plesure of the Supereme,
without selfish motives, one can be liberated from the from the law of Karma(action and reaction) and attain transcendental knowledge of self and the Supreme.

CHAPTER FOUR

Transcendental Knowledge

Transcendental knowledge--the spiritual knowledge of the soul,of God,and of their relationship
--is both purifying and liberating. Such knowledge is the fruit of selfless devotional action (karma-yoga). The Lord explains the remote history of the Gita,
the purpose and significance of His periodic descents to the material world,and the neccessity of approching a guru,a realized teacher.

CHAPTER FIVE

Karma-yoga--Action in Krsna Consciouness

Outwardly performing all action but inwardly renouncing their fruits,
the wise man, pruified by the fire of transcendental knowledge, attains peace, detachment, forbearance, spritual vision and bliss.
